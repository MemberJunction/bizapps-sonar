Why this product, why now

The category is commoditized at the score and wide open at the act. Five wedges, each independently defensible.

1

Configurable over the MJ graph

Anchor on any entity, wire in any related entities, run many concurrent models. Members, donors, volunteers, chapters — one engine, different rubrics. Nobody else does multi-model, multi-entity, per-org authoring.

2

MJ is the data fabric

The expensive, fragile part of every competitor — stitching CRM + finance + LMS + community + email into one view — is a precondition on MJ, not a project. We read the unified graph; they sell you the ETL first.

3

Explainable by construction

A score is a weighted sum of named factors, so it decomposes for free: "73, down 12 — −15 newsletter decay, −22 cert lapsed." The narrative is what makes staff act. Black-box BI can't hand you that.

4

Cross-tenant calibration

"Is 6% community participation good?" is unanswerable in one tenant and learnable across a network. Benchmarked risk models and transfer learning on what interventions work. NDAs and lack of concentration lock everyone else out.

5

The action layer, with honest lift

Surface the cohort, draft the outreach, fire it as an Action — and measure lift against an automatic holdout. Most of the category won't hold out a control group. Doing it is the trust-builder and the basis for performance pricing.

+

Packaged as an OpenApp

Engine, entities, starter factor library, model templates, the config/dashboard agent, and dashboards — one OpenApp. Installable on any MJ deployment; layered on BC SaaS for the calibration network.

The one big idea

Configuration is data, not code.

Models, factors, rubrics, windows, bands, write-back rules, and action playbooks are all rows. Code is the engine that interprets them — plus the escape-hatch Actions for arbitrary logic. Declarative factors compile to set-based SQL; Action-backed factors (code or CodeSmith-generated Runtime Actions) handle the weird 20%. Both satisfy one Factor contract, so the rubric engine and the explainability view never care which kind they hold.
